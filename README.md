I am student at Yale University studying Applied Physics and Economics. I appreciate the combination of theory and research alongside practical applications that both of these fields provide.

In my free time, I am passionate about using technology to solve problems in everyday life. I first learned to code in Swift and have created projects for fun. In my projects, I focus especially on design, which I believe is critical for software to be used as simply and productively as possible. I love to read and stay up-to-date on current events, business, and geopolitics.

Looking ahead, I aim to continue learning about the healthcare sector - the U.S.'s largest, and one beset by administrative complexity - as I work on Argentiv Health. I also see potential in the modernization of infrastructure as the AI boom and electrification place strain on the electric grid.

## Junior Economic Club App

The Junior Economic Club (JEC) is a student‑run, U.S.-based nonprofit with chapters in four countries. To support the organization’s growth and streamline the member experience, I developed a mobile app that supports event registration; personalized messaging via SMS, push notifications, and email; a member directory; and additional member tools.  

The app is available on the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/junior-economic-club/id6473089216)

## Help Me Recycle

Help Me Recycle is an iOS app that guides U.S. consumers on recycling. Users can scan How2Recycle labels on consumer goods packaging and receive localized recycling guidance. I was awarded a Certificate of Special Congressional Recognition.

Key features:

- Label scanning powered by a custom image recognition model trained with Create ML.  
- On-device inference with a model that works in challenging lighting and on dark backgrounds.  
- A built-in recycling guide summarizing local recycling options.
